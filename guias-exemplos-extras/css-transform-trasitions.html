Welcome to the Semantic HTML Guide

The <header> tag represents introductory content or a set of navigation links. It typically contains a logo, heading, and sometimes a brief introduction.

The <nav> tag is used for navigation links. It provides a way to navigate the site and is commonly used for menus or tables of contents:

  • Introduction
  • Main Content
  • Conclusion

Introduction

The <main> tag is used for the dominant content of the body. This is where the main, unique content of a document resides.

What is Semantic HTML?

The <section> tag defines a thematic grouping of content, typically with a heading. It is used to break up content into sections that are logically related.

Understanding <article>

The <article> tag represents self-contained content that can be independently distributed or reused. Common examples include blog posts, news articles, or forum posts.

Using <aside>

The <aside> tag is for content related to the main content but not essential to it, such as sidebars, callout boxes, or related links.

Conclusion

The <footer> tag is used for footer content, such as copyright information, links to related documents, or contact details. It often appears at the bottom of a page or section.
